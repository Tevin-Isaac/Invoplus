How it works

Four steps, one atomic settlement.

01
List

A business uploads an unpaid invoice. It's risk-scored and graded A–D.

02
Bid

Financiers submit sealed bids, hidden from the seller and each other.

03
Fund

The winning bid pays the seller early, at the agreed advance rate.

04
Repay

The debtor pays at maturity; the financier collects principal plus spread.

Why Canton

The guarantees aren't features. They're properties of the ledger.

Sealed bids stay sealed.
Each bid is a contract only the bidder and platform can see. The seller isn't an observer, so the ledger physically cannot reveal bid amounts.
Settlement is atomic.
Choosing the winner, funding the seller, and transferring the obligation happen in a single transaction. No in-between state to exploit.
No double financing.
A ledger key on each invoice makes a second listing of the same invoice fail at the protocol level.
Business model

A fee on financed volume.

We take a small percentage of every invoice financed through the platform. No subscriptions, no listing fees. We earn only when a seller gets funded, so our incentive is aligned with volume flowing through the marketplace.

Two sides, one flywheel: more sellers attract more financiers, tighter pricing attracts more sellers.
